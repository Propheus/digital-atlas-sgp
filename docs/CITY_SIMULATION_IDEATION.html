TL;DR

Yes, we can build city-level simulation — but at Levels 1–2 (scenario scorer + equilibrium sim), not at Level 3 (full agent-based model).

SimMobility already exists at SMART/SUTD for SGP and is used by LTA. The smart play is not to rebuild it but to position our 460-feature + 64-dim + micrograph substrate as the richest grounded geography layer for SGP, then layer:

  • • A hex-level scenario scorer (ships in 2 weeks, real planning value)
  • • A hex-level equilibrium simulator (ships in 1–2 months, novel at this granularity)
  • • An LLM-agent research spike (differentiator: nobody has our prompt grounding)
  • • A SimMobility integration path for credibility at the ABM tier

Framing: not a "city simulator" but a simulation substrate + targeted scenario tools. Realer, achievable, defensible.

4. The Ambition Ladder — Level by Level

L1 Static Scenario Scorer feasible — 2 weeks

What: User specifies an intervention (new MRT at hex X, +5K residents to subzone Y, rezone Z to commercial). Modify feature inputs, re-run the 24 XGBoost predictors, show ripple effects via the influence graph (one or few hops).

Mechanics: intervention vector → perturbed features → XGBoost predictions → delta vs baseline → propagate via graph
Honest namePartial-equilibrium delta engine, not a simulator
Good forInteractive UX, policy pitches, what-if demos
Bad forAnything with temporal feedback loops
UsesExisting 24 XGBoost JSONs + 391 features + graph
Example output"Adding MRT at hex X → +12% commerce density within 600m; cafe shifts most (+18%), hospitality +8%"
Effort2 weeks (80% of logic exists in scenario_sim at subzone)

L2 Hex-Level Equilibrium Simulator feasible — 1-2 months

What: Add demand-supply loops. Each iteration: Huff gravity allocates consumer spend across hexes → place densities update → amenity attractiveness changes → re-allocate. Converge to a new steady state.

Mechanics: Huff allocation + entropy maximization + iterative place-density update. Output: new equilibrium hex state + cross-hex flows.
Honest nameStatic equilibrium with cross-hex feedback (no time axis)
Good forRetail impact, MRT ROI, amenity policy sizing
Bad forDaily / seasonal dynamics (converges, doesn't animate)
UsesBundle + proxy OD matrix (synthesized from pop × transit × walkability)
Example output"New MRT station: converged steady state has +8% equilibrium employment in 3 subzones, −3% in CBD overflow, cafe market shifts 11%"
Effort1–2 months; scenario_sim has the math at subzone level — we'd rebuild at hex

L3 Full Agent-Based Simulation don't rebuild — integrate with SimMobility

What: 5.9M synthetic agents, demographics, jobs, daily activity schedules, trip chains, mode choice. A full ABM.

Reality check: SimMobility already exists, calibrated on SGP, used by LTA. Rebuilding it is 2–3 FTE-years for something that ships on day 0 of the partnership.
Our playOffer the bundle as SimMobility's rich geography layer
Value-addOur 460 features, GCN-64 similarity, and micrographs are better than what most ABMs consume as input
Partnership pathSMART / SUTD / LTA — joint publication potential
Political outcomeCredible "our research integrates with established SGP agent-based simulation"
EffortMostly relationship-building, integration scripting. Months, not years.

L4 LLM-Agent Generative Simulation research spike — 2-3 months, genuinely novel

What: 1K–10K synthetic residents as LLM personas (young professional in Tiong Bahru, retiree in Bedok, tourist in Marina Bay). Each simulated day, each agent picks 3–5 hexes to visit based on persona + hex embeddings + weather + events.

Why this is compelling for us specifically: Our 174K LLM-classified places + 42K micrographs + 12 category contexts produce best-in-class prompt grounding for agent decisions. Very few cities in the world have this substrate.
OutputsPer-hex footfall, flow patterns, substitution under intervention
InterventionsModify hex embedding → agents re-plan → measure emergent effect
Cost (naive)10K agents × 5 decisions/day × 30 days = 1.5M LLM calls/month of sim ≈ $1.5K
Cost (distilled)Train smaller classifier from LLM outputs, drop cost 100×
FeasibilityYes for research demo, borderline for production without distillation
Research contribution"Generative Urban Agents grounded in rich place embeddings" — genuinely publishable

L5 Neural World Model out of scope today

What: GNN/Transformer that predicts hex-state(t+1) given hex-state(t) + action vector.

The killer problem: we don't have training data. Neural world models learn from history. We have one snapshot.

Future path only. Revisit when we have 2–3 more annual snapshots, or a SimMobility partnership yields synthetic trajectories at scale.

5. The Recommended Strategy

Don't build "a city simulator." Build three targeted artifacts under a credible simulation umbrella.

Artifact 1 — HexScenarioEngine

Level: L1 (static scorer)

Time: 2 weeks

Stack: FastAPI on top of 24 XGBoost JSONs + influence graph

Endpoint: POST /scenario → delta map

Why: Fast demo, interactive map UX, immediate planning value

Artifact 2 — HexEquilibriumSim

Level: L2 (Huff equilibrium)

Time: 1–2 months

Stack: Iterative solver on hex graph with Huff + entropy-max

Output: converged state + inter-hex flows

Why: Real planning value; novel at hex granularity for SGP

Artifact 3 — GenerativeUrbanAgents

Level: L4 (LLM agents)

Time: 2–3 months (parallel research track)

Stack: 1K LLM personas + our embedding/micrograph grounding

Output: emergent footfall, flow patterns

Why: Genuine research differentiation, paper potential

Plus: a SimMobility integration path for L3 credibility. Don't rebuild what SMART/SUTD already runs.

6. Questions to Answer Before Starting

Who is the target user? PR/policy demos → Artifact 1 is enough. Urban research → Artifact 2. Product differentiation / "AI city twin" narrative → Artifact 3.
What's the simulation's purpose? Intervention testing (Artifact 1), forecasting (Artifact 2), optimization (Artifact 2), or research novelty (Artifact 3)? Each dictates different depth.
What's "good enough"? A sim that's wrong in details but right in trends (Artifact 1-2) is more valuable than one that strives for perfection but can't be validated (Artifact 5).
Do we have ground truth? Without validation data, a sim is narrative. What can we calibrate against? Our XGBoost predictions, SingStat projections, LTA passenger flows, HITS survey, SimMobility output.
What's the partnership strategy? Going alone limits us to L1/L2/L4. SimMobility integration opens L3 and L5 within 6–12 months.

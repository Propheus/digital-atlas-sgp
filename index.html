Micrograph: How It Works

Every place gets a star-graph with up to 25 anchors in 4 tiers:

T1: Transit

MRT stations within walk-time budget. Flow-weighted by ridership estimate.

T2: Competitors

Same-category places. Ranked by magnitude (reviews × rating) or walk-time.

T3: Complementary

Adjacent categories (bakery near cafe, hawker near office). Synergy signal.

T4: Demand Magnets

Offices, malls, schools, HDB clusters. Generate foot traffic.

Model Results

Finding 1: Physical structure predicts place density (R²=0.773) — population, roads, zoning, transit strongly determine how many places exist.
Finding 2: Physical structure alone cannot predict composition (R²≈0) — whether an area has more cafes vs clinics is market-driven, not infrastructure-driven.
Finding 3: Knowing partial composition helps predict the rest (R²=0.18-0.53) — inter-category relationships are real but noisy.

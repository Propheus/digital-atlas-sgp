Build Timeline

March 17
Overture Maps POI extraction
Downloaded 340,857 Overture POIs for Jakarta (Jabodetabek) using DuckDB on Azure server. Deployed to 12-server fleet for Google Maps curation — 96.5% find rate.
March 17
Singapore places discovery
Scraped 79,257 Singapore places across 12 Azure servers (24 workers each). Extracted name, address, category, rating, phone, website from Google Maps.
March 18
Places curation & dedup
Deduped by geohash9 + name → 68,269 unique places. Assigned 12-char alphanumeric IDs. Normalized names (caps, emojis, SEO junk removed). Clipped to GADM Singapore boundary → 66,851 final.
March 18
Category taxonomy
Built 24 main categories + 165 place types. Identified 232 brands. Tagged luxury places. Iterative refinement — multiple passes to reduce "Other" from 8,507 → 0.
March 18
Data collection
Assembled 15 data layers (1.9 GB): Singstat census 2025, URA Master Plan (113K parcels), LTA transit (5.2K bus stops, 231 MRT), OSM roads (551K edges), OSM buildings (126K), HDB resale (227K txns), ACRA entities (2M), SFA eating establishments (34K).
March 19
Feature engineering
19-step pipeline → 202-dimension subzone feature vectors. Demographics, dwelling types, HDB prices, road density, land use zoning, transit accessibility, amenity distances, place composition, brand density, government cross-reference. 30/30 validation tests passed.
March 19
Graph construction
Subzone adjacency (332 nodes, 1,001 edges), co-location PMI (155 types, 5,397 edges), transit connectivity (139 MRT-connected subzones, 914 edges).
March 19
Model training
Tested GCN-MLP (R²=0.597) and XGBoost (R²=0.970 leaky, 0.773 strict). Key insight: physical structure predicts density (R²=0.77) but not composition. Gap analysis computed for all 332 subzones × 24 categories.
March 19
Micrograph pipeline v1
Built per-place star-graphs for 12 categories. 4-tier system: Transit (MRT), Competitors, Complementary, Demand Magnets. 72/72 validation tests passed.
March 20
LLM reclassification
All 66,851 places run through Gemini 2.0 Flash via OpenRouter. 8-thread parallel processing, ~3,500 places/min. 660 places reclassified (e.g., "King of Fried Rice" moved from Cafe to Hawker & Street Food).
March 20
Micrograph pipeline v2
Complete rewrite with quality-first rules: T1=MRT only, T2=actual cafe competitors with 10+ reviews, T3=bakeries/bookstores with 15+ reviews, T4=malls/supermarkets/hawkers with 30+ reviews. Avg anchors dropped from 22 → 10.7 (fewer but meaningful).
March 20
App deployment
Adapted NYC Digital Atlas v2 codebase for SGP. All text, metrics, coordinates, descriptions customized. Deployed on rwm-server port 18067. 44K places on map across 6 category groups.

Micrograph v2: Quality Anchors

T1 Transit: MRT stations only. Walk-time weighted by sigmoid decay. Max 3 per place.
T2 Competitors: Actual cafes/coffee shops with 10+ reviews. Ranked by magnitude (reviews × rating). Max 6.
T3 Complementary: Bakeries, bookstores, coworking spaces with 15+ reviews. Real synergy signals. Max 5.
T4 Demand Magnets: Shopping malls (200+ reviews), hawker centres, universities, hospitals, supermarkets with 30+ reviews. Proven foot traffic. Max 4.
Result: Average 10.7 anchors per place (was 22 in v1). Fewer but every anchor is meaningful with real review evidence.

Key Insight

Physical urban structure predicts how many places exist (R²=0.77) but not what kind (R²≈0.18). Composition is market-driven — cafe vs restaurant vs salon is an entrepreneur decision, not determined by roads and zoning. The density model IS the real Digital Atlas signal.
